1st Kup — TAGB (ITF) Taekwondo

Black Stripe to Black Belt

Your complete pre-grading revision guide, checklist, and resources for 1st Kup. Tick off every practical item before grading day — no excuses!

Congratulations, Black Stripes! 🥋 You are standing at the threshold of the most significant achievement in Taekwondo — Black Belt (1st Dan). This is the culmination of years of dedication, discipline, and hard work. The 1st Kup grading is the final coloured belt examination before Black Belt, and it demands the very best of everything you have learned.

At this level, examiners expect mastery of Choong-Moo (the final coloured belt pattern — 30 movements), all previous patterns performed to a high standard, advanced sparring skills across all formats, power destruction, and a comprehensive knowledge of Korean terminology. Your technique must be sharp, your stances solid, your attitude exemplary.

🚨 This is Your Final Step — Make It Count
Black Belt is not just a belt — it is a statement of character, commitment, and capability. Examiners will be assessing not just your physical skills, but your spirit, focus, and attitude throughout the entire grading. Every movement, every bow, every response matters.

💪 Training Commitment is Critical
At Black Stripe level, training once or twice a week is not enough. Aim for multiple sessions each week, supplemented by solo practice at home. Focus on pattern precision, sparring combinations, and conditioning. Use the training videos below to study technique and timing.

🥊 Sparring at Its Most Demanding
1st Kup sparring requires advanced timing, control, combination work, and the ability to perform under pressure. You must demonstrate 3-step, 2-step, and 1-step sparring, semi-free sparring, and free sparring — all to a high standard. Regular sparring practice is essential.

Use the grading video provided to support your training. Practice alongside your classes to refine techniques, build confidence, and fully understand what will be expected on grading day. The Black Belt is within reach — train hard, stay focused, and give everything you have. 🥋⬛

🥊 1-Step Sparring — Advanced (Ilbo Matsoki)

At 1st Kup, Black Stripes develop their own 1-step sparring techniques using the following five categories (not in a set order). Both attacker and defender start in parallel ready stance. The attacker steps forward with the right leg and punches with the right hand; on the second occasion, with the left leg and left hand.

  1. Hand only
  2. Foot only
  3. Hand and foot
  4. Foot and hand
  5. Jumping

🔄 Choong-Moo — 30 Movements ▼
Meaning: Named after the great Admiral Yi Soon-Sin of the Yi Dynasty (1592 AD). The pattern ends with a left-hand attack because it is said he was given no chance to show his loyalty to the king. The 30 movements represent his outstanding contribution to the development of the Korean navy.
Starting Position: Parallel ready stance (Narani Chumbi Sogi)
1. Knifehand guarding block (L-stance)Move left foot to B, right L-stance toward B, middle guarding block knife-hand
2. Side piercing kick + punch (walking stance)Side piercing kick to B right foot; lower right foot to B, left walking stance toward B, middle punch right fist
3. Knifehand guarding block (L-stance)Move right foot to A, left L-stance toward A, middle guarding block knife-hand
4. Side piercing kick + punch (walking stance)Side piercing kick to A left foot; lower left foot to A, right walking stance toward A, middle punch left fist
5. Upward punch (walking stance)Move left foot to D, right walking stance toward D, upward punch right fist
6. Upward punch (walking stance)Move right foot to D, left walking stance toward D, upward punch left fist
7. Knifehand guarding block (L-stance)Move left foot to C, right L-stance toward D, middle guarding block knife-hand
8. Flying side piercing kick (L-stance)Jump and execute flying side piercing kick to D right foot; land in right L-stance toward D, middle guarding block knife-hand
9. Knifehand strike (L-stance)Move left foot to C, right L-stance toward D, high inward knifehand strike right hand
10. Pushing block + punch (walking stance)Move right foot to D, right walking stance toward D, pushing block left palm; middle punch right fist
11. Sweeping block (L-stance)Move left foot to C, right L-stance toward D, middle sweeping block right forearm
12. Reverse knifehand strike (L-stance)Move right foot to D, left L-stance toward D, high reverse knifehand strike right hand
13. Knifehand guarding block (L-stance)Move left foot to B, right L-stance toward B, middle guarding block knife-hand
14. Vertical kick + punch (walking stance)Vertical kick to B right foot; lower right foot to B, left walking stance toward B, middle punch right fist
15. Knifehand guarding block (L-stance)Move right foot to A, left L-stance toward A, middle guarding block knife-hand
16. Vertical kick + punch (walking stance)Vertical kick to A left foot; lower left foot to A, right walking stance toward A, middle punch left fist
17. Twin upset punch (walking stance)Move left foot to D, right walking stance toward D, twin upset punch
18. Twin upset punch (walking stance)Move right foot to D, left walking stance toward D, twin upset punch
19. Knifehand guarding block (L-stance)Move left foot to C, right L-stance toward D, middle guarding block knife-hand
20. Flying side piercing kick (L-stance)Jump and execute flying side piercing kick to D right foot; land in right L-stance toward D, middle guarding block knife-hand
21. Knifehand strike (L-stance)Move left foot to C, right L-stance toward D, high inward knifehand strike right hand
22. Pushing block + punch (walking stance)Move right foot to D, right walking stance toward D, pushing block left palm; middle punch right fist
23. Sweeping block (L-stance)Move left foot to C, right L-stance toward D, middle sweeping block right forearm
24. Reverse knifehand strike (L-stance)Move right foot to D, left L-stance toward D, high reverse knifehand strike right hand
25. Twin foot kick (one-leg stance)Pull left foot to right knee joint, right one-leg stance toward D; execute twin foot kick to D
26. Knifehand guarding block (L-stance)Land left foot to C, right L-stance toward D, middle guarding block knife-hand
27. Twisting kick + punch (walking stance)Twisting kick to D right foot; lower right foot to D, left walking stance toward D, middle punch right fist
28. Flying high kick (walking stance)Jump and execute flying high kick to D; land in right walking stance toward D
29. Upward punch (walking stance)Move left foot to D, right walking stance toward D, upward punch right fist
30. Upward punch (walking stance)Move right foot to D, left walking stance toward D, upward punch left fist. Note: the pattern ends with a left-hand attack.
END:Bring the right foot back to a ready posture
