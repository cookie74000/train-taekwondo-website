Meet Dee

Meet Dee Smith — 18 years old, 7 years into her Taekwondo journey, and one of the most dedicated students in our dojang. From her first competition as a colour belt to earning her 2nd Dan Black Belt, Dee has shown the kind of commitment, resilience, and heart that makes a true martial artist. She now gives back to the club as an assistant instructor, helping the next generation find their feet on the mat.

"Just keep going — every session makes you better, even when it doesn't feel like it."

— Dee Smith, February 2026
In Her Own Words
What's your favourite kick?
Turning kick — it's the one I feel most confident with in sparring.
What's your favourite pattern?
Toi-Gye — I love the power movements in it.
What's been your biggest challenge in Taekwondo?
Staying consistent when life gets busy. There were times I wanted to quit, but I'm so glad I didn't.
What's your proudest Taekwondo moment?
Getting my 2nd Dan. It felt like everything I'd worked for finally came together.
Which tenet of Taekwondo means the most to you?
Perseverance. It's the one I've had to use the most — on the mat and off it.
What would you say to someone thinking about starting Taekwondo?
Just try it. The first session is the hardest — after that, you'll wonder why you waited so long.
What do you enjoy most about being an assistant instructor?
Seeing the younger students improve and get excited about their training. It reminds me of when I started.

A Note from Mr Cook

Dee is exactly the kind of student that makes instructing so rewarding. She has worked incredibly hard over seven years, never taking shortcuts, and always giving 100% whether she's competing, grading, or helping with the younger students. Watching her grow from a nervous colour belt into a confident 2nd Dan and assistant instructor has been a real privilege. She is a credit to herself, her family, and to Train Taekwondo Schools.

We are proud to name Dee our February 2026 Student Spotlight. Well deserved, Dee — keep going!
